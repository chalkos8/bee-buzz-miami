Did You Know?

Fascinating Bee Facts

🌍

One Third of Our Food

Bees pollinate approximately 1 in 3 bites of food we eat, including fruits, vegetables, nuts, and seeds.

🏃

Amazing Navigators

Honeybees fly up to 15 mph and can navigate as far as 5 miles from their hive, using the sun as a compass.

💬

The Waggle Dance

Scout bees communicate flower locations through a figure-8 "waggle dance" that encodes precise distance and direction.

👑

The Queen Bee

A queen can live 3–5 years and lay up to 2,000 eggs per day — more than her own body weight!

🍯

Honey Never Expires

Archaeologists found 3,000-year-old honey in Egyptian tombs that was still perfectly edible — honey is eternal.

🌡️

Climate Controlled

Bees keep their hive at exactly 95°F year-round, cooling it by fanning or warming it by clustering — remarkable precision.

Florida Species Guide

Common Bees in South Florida

🐝

European Honey Bee

Apis mellifera

The most common managed bee in Florida. Gentle, productive, and excellent pollinators. The gold standard of beekeeping.

✅ Generally Docile
🐝

Africanized Honey Bee

Apis mellifera scutellata

Found throughout South Florida. Visually identical to European bees but dramatically more defensive. Require expert handling.

⚠️ Approach with Caution
🐝

Bumblebee

Bombus spp.

Round, fuzzy native bees that are critical pollinators. Rarely sting unless directly threatened. Nest in the ground or cavities.

✅ Very Docile
🐝

Carpenter Bee

Xylocopa spp.

Large shiny bees that bore into wood to nest. Males are territorial but stingless; females rarely sting. Can damage wood structures.

ℹ️ Mostly Harmless

Our Story

From a Backyard Hive to Miami's Premier Beekeeper

It started with a single hive in a Coconut Grove backyard in 2008. Founder Carlos Mendez, a third-generation Cuban-American farmer, inherited his grandfather's passion for bees and natural living. What began as a hobby quickly became a calling.

After rescuing a neighbor's backyard swarm and realizing how few humane options existed in Miami, Carlos left his engineering career to dedicate himself fully to bee conservation and education.

Today, Bee Buzz Miami manages over 200 hives across South Florida, has relocated more than 1,500 colonies, and produces some of the finest raw honey in the Southeast — all while staying true to our founding mission: protect the bees, protect the planet.
